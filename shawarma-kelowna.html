What is shawarma (and what makes ours different)?

Shawarma is a Levantine and Middle Eastern technique — meat marinated in a spice blend, stacked vertically on a rotating spit, and slow-roasted over heat so the outer layer crisps while the interior stays juicy. The outside is shaved off in thin strips as it cooks, which is why every bite of shawarma you order is cooked differently than the one before it. At Basha we marinate our chicken for a minimum of 12 hours in cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, garlic, and lemon, and our beef gets a deeper blend with cinnamon, black pepper, and allspice. No pre-seasoned factory meat. No microwaves.

Chicken shawarma vs beef shawarma vs donair — what's the difference?

All three are cooked on vertical spits, but the meats and spice profiles are different. Chicken shawarma uses marinated chicken thighs (juicier than breast) with a lighter, brighter spice blend. Beef shawarma uses sliced beef with a deeper, warmer spice profile. Donair uses spiced ground beef — it's denser, more crumbly, and typically paired with a garlic sauce profile in our Maritime-Canadian style. If you've never had all three, order the mixed wrap and decide for yourself.
