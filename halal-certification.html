What halal means at Basha

Halal isn't just "no pork." It means every animal protein traces back to a certified halal abattoir where the slaughter follows zabiha rules — a swift cut to the throat with a sharp blade while the animal faces Mecca, accompanied by a blessing. Beyond that, it means: no haram ingredients in seasonings or sauces, separate utensils and storage where required, and continuous chain-of-custody documentation from supplier to plate.

Our commitments

  • ◆All beef and chicken sourced from halal-certified Canadian suppliers with full chain-of-custody paperwork.
  • ◆No pork, no alcohol, anywhere on the premises.
  • ◆Dedicated halal fryers — vegetarian items don't share oil with non-halal cookware (because we have no non-halal cookware).
  • ◆Spice blends and sauces are individually verified halal at the ingredient level.
  • ◆Annual re-certification with a recognized halal authority.

Frequently asked

Q: Are vegetarian items halal too?
Yes. Even our vegetarian falafel is prepared in a fully halal kitchen. There is no cross-contact with non-halal items because we don't serve any non-halal items.
Q: Is the meat zabiha?
Yes. All beef and chicken comes from zabiha-certified suppliers.
Q: Can I see the certificate?
Yes — ask any staff member at the counter. We're happy to show you.
Q: Who issues your certification?
A recognized Canadian halal certification authority. Re-audited annually.
