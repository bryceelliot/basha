What makes a restaurant 'authentic Lebanese'?

Authentic Lebanese cooking starts with the basics done right: olive oil that's actually fresh, parsley that arrives daily, tahini ground from sesame seeds (not from a generic supplier), spices toasted before grinding. The donair-and-shawarma vertical-spit technique came out of Lebanon in the early 20th century. So did hummus as we know it today. Tabbouleh — the way Beirut makes it, with parsley as the star, not just bulgur — is also distinctly Lebanese.
