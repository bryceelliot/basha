Kebab in Kelowna — The Real Thing

Kebab is one word for many things. Doner kebab is what spinning vertical-spit roast meat is called in Turkey. Shawarma is what it's called in the Levant. Donair is what Halifax called it after Greek-Canadian Peter Gamoulakos brought it to Canada in 1973. At Basha we serve all three traditions under one roof — same spit, different recipes.

Doner kebab vs shawarma vs donair

All three are vertical-spit-roasted meat. Doner kebab (Turkish original): seasoned beef or lamb, sliced thin, served in pita with vegetables and yogurt sauce. Shawarma (Levantine): marinated chicken or beef with cumin/coriander/paprika, served in pita with garlic sauce and pickled vegetables. Donair (Canadian, from Halifax 1973): spiced ground beef, savoury garlic sauce on the West Coast, sweet condensed-milk sauce in the Maritimes. We do shawarma and donair daily; doner kebab on request for catering orders.
