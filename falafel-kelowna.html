What is real falafel?

Falafel is one of the oldest dishes in the Middle East — chickpeas (or fava beans) ground with parsley, cilantro, garlic, cumin, and coriander, formed into balls or patties, and deep-fried. The exterior crisps to a deep golden-brown; the interior stays tender and herb-bright. At Basha we grind our chickpeas same-day, season our mix in small batches, and fry to order so every falafel is hot and crispy when it hits your wrap.
