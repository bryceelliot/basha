How to Warm Pita Without Making It a Cracker

TL;DR

30 seconds in a dry skillet on medium-high, both sides. No oil, no butter. The pita puffs slightly and stays soft. Microwave doesn't work.

Why most home pita is bad

Most North Americans either microwave their pita (which makes it gummy then leathery) or skip warming it (which makes it taste raw). Both wreck the eating experience.

The skillet method (best)

Dry cast-iron pan on medium-high heat. Pita on, count to 30. Flip, count to 30. Done. Soft, slightly puffed, hot through. This is how every Lebanese restaurant does it.

The grill method

Outdoor BBQ on medium. 20 seconds per side. Adds light char marks and a faint smoky note.

The oven method

Stack pita on a sheet pan, splash of water on top, foil cover, 350°F for 5 minutes. Good for 6+ pitas at once.

Why microwave fails

Microwaves heat water content from inside out. The pita gets gummy first, then suddenly leathery. There's no good window.
