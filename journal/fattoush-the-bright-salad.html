Fattoush — Lebanon's Bright, Sumac-Bright Salad

TL;DR

Fattoush is a Lebanese salad with crispy fried pita, sumac, and a sharp lemon-pomegranate-molasses dressing. The pita stays crisp when you serve it on top, not mixed in. Sumac is non-negotiable.

The vegetables

2 Persian cucumbers diced, 2 Roma tomatoes diced, 1/2 head romaine torn, 1/2 cup radishes sliced thin, 1/2 cup red onion thinly sliced, 1/2 cup parsley chopped, 1/4 cup mint chopped, 1/4 cup green onions chopped.

The crispy pita

2 stale pita rounds cut into squares. Fry in olive oil over medium heat until deeply golden — about 4 minutes. Drain on paper towels. This is the signature texture of fattoush, and it MUST be done right. Stale pita crisps; fresh pita gets soggy.

The dressing

1/4 cup olive oil, 3 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tbsp pomegranate molasses, 1 tbsp sumac, 1/2 tsp salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper, 1 small clove garlic minced. Whisk.

The assembly

Toss vegetables with most of the dressing. Plate. THEN add the crispy pita on top + extra dressing + a final dusting of sumac. Serve immediately.

Sumac, the secret

Sumac is the dried ground berry of a Mediterranean shrub. It tastes like lemon zest crossed with cranberry — tart, bright, slightly tannic. There is no substitute. Buy it from a Middle Eastern grocer or order online. Without sumac, it isn't fattoush.
