The Basha Donair Spice Blend (Approximate)

TL;DR

A close approximation of the donair spice blend: oregano, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, black pepper, salt — mixed with bread crumbs and beaten into ground beef before forming on the spit.

The base spice mix

2 tbsp oregano, 2 tsp paprika, 2 tsp garlic powder, 2 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp cayenne, 1 tsp black pepper, 2 tsp salt. Mix dry. This is enough for 2 lb of ground beef.

The meat

2 lb lean ground beef (15% fat or higher — leaner doesn't bind on the spit), 1 cup fine bread crumbs (to hold the meat together when sliced thin), 1/2 cup ice water.

The technique

Mix everything in a stand mixer with the paddle attachment for 10 minutes. The meat needs to be punished — over-mixing is the goal here, not the enemy. The proteins denature and bind, which is what makes donair meat slice cleanly into thin curls instead of crumbling.

The cook

Form into a tight loaf shape and roast at 200°F for 4 hours, then crank to 425°F for 15 minutes to crisp the outside. Cool fully (overnight in the fridge is best). Slice thin against the grain — that's the closest home approximation of vertical-spit donair.
