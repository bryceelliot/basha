How to Marinate Shawarma Like a Restaurant

TL;DR

For real shawarma at home: chicken thighs (not breast), 12+ hour marinade in yogurt + cumin + coriander + paprika + turmeric + garlic + lemon, then high heat. The yogurt and time are non-negotiable.

The marinade

Mix in a bowl: 1 cup plain yogurt (Greek or Balkan-style works best), 3 tbsp olive oil, juice of 1 lemon, 6 cloves garlic minced, 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp coriander, 2 tsp paprika, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, optional 1/2 tsp cardamom. That's the chicken version. For beef, swap turmeric for 1 tsp cinnamon and 1/2 tsp allspice.

The cut

Boneless skinless chicken thighs only. Breast dries out. Cut into 2-inch chunks before marinating — this lets the marinade penetrate.

The time

Minimum 12 hours, ideally 24. The yogurt slowly tenderizes the meat (the lactic acid breaks down proteins) while the spices penetrate. Less than 12 hours and the spices stay on the surface.

The cook

High heat. A cast-iron pan or BBQ grill. You want the outer surface to crisp and char while the inside stays juicy. 4-5 minutes per side, until the outside is dark golden. Rest 3 minutes before slicing thin.

The serve

Warm pita, fresh garlic sauce (toum — see our previous post), pickled turnips, tomatoes, lettuce, onions. Wrap tight.
