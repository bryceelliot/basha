The Story of the Vertical Spit — How Donair Got to Kelowna

TL;DR

The vertical spit was invented in 19th-century Turkey, where it was called 'döner kebab'. It spread through Lebanon as 'shawarma'. Greek-Canadian Peter Gamoulakos adapted it to Canada in 1970s Halifax, creating the donair. It reached Kelowna in the 1980s–90s and remains a regional favourite.

Bursa, Turkey, 1860s

The vertical spit cooking method was first documented in Bursa, Turkey, by an Ottoman chef named İskender Efendi. He took the traditional horizontal kebab spit and turned it vertical, allowing fat to drip down and self-baste the meat as it rotated. The dish became known as 'döner kebab' (literally 'rotating roast').

Beirut, early 20th century

The technique spread south through the Levant, reaching Beirut and Damascus in the early 1900s. There it took on a new identity as 'shawarma' — using marinated thin-sliced meat (often chicken, beef, or lamb) and pairing it with garlic sauce, tahini, and pita. The shawarma version became the dominant form across the Arab world.

Halifax, 1973

Greek-Canadian immigrant Peter Gamoulakos opened a restaurant in Halifax serving a version of the döner he'd seen growing up. Canadian customers found the original lamb too gamey, so he switched to spiced ground beef. He paired it with a sweet condensed-milk-and-vinegar sauce that locals loved. The dish became known as 'donair' — a phonetic spelling of 'döner' for English-speaking customers. Halifax declared it the city's official food in 2015.

Out west

Through the 1980s and 90s, donair spread west with the help of Maritime expatriates, university students, and immigrant entrepreneurs. By the late 1990s, donair shops were opening in Calgary, Edmonton, Vancouver, and the Okanagan. The Western Canadian version evolved away from the sweet Maritime sauce — most BC and Alberta shops now serve a savoury garlic sauce as the default, and customers expect a heartier, less-sweet flavour profile.

Basha in Kelowna

Basha brought a Lebanese-tradition take on the Western Canadian donair to Kelowna. We make our spiced ground beef in-house, slow-roast it on a vertical spit, and serve it with our family's garlic sauce recipe. Same technique that started in 1860s Turkey, adapted by Greek-Canadians in 1970s Halifax, refined by us in Rutland.
