Tabbouleh Done Right — Parsley, Not Bulgur

TL;DR

Real Lebanese tabbouleh is a parsley salad with bulgur, not a bulgur salad with parsley. Use 4 bunches parsley to 1/4 cup bulgur. Tomato, mint, lemon, olive oil, salt. That's it.

The right ratio

North American tabbouleh tends to be 50% bulgur, 30% parsley, 20% other. That's wrong. Real Lebanese tabbouleh is 80% parsley, 10% bulgur, 10% tomato + mint + onion. The bulgur is a textural accent, not a base.

The parsley

4 large bunches of flat-leaf parsley. Wash, dry thoroughly (a salad spinner is non-negotiable), and chop FINE. Knife work matters — mushy parsley ruins it.

The bulgur

1/4 cup fine-grade bulgur (#1, the smallest), soaked in cold water for 15 min, then drained and squeezed. Don't cook it.

Everything else

2 ripe tomatoes diced small (1/4 inch), 1/4 cup fresh mint chopped, 2 tbsp finely diced white onion, juice of 2 lemons, 1/4 cup olive oil, 1 tsp salt, fresh cracked pepper.

The toss

Mix in a bowl. Eat within 1 hour — tabbouleh wilts. The high lemon-to-oil ratio is what makes it bright.
