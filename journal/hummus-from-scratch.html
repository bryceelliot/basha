Hummus From Scratch — The Lebanese Way

TL;DR

Real Lebanese hummus uses dried chickpeas (not canned), peeled after cooking, blended hot, with twice as much tahini as you'd think. The result is impossibly smooth and bright.

Start with dried chickpeas

Soak 1 cup dried chickpeas overnight with 1/2 tsp baking soda. The baking soda is critical — it raises the pH and makes the chickpea skins easier to peel after cooking. Skipping this is why home hummus tastes grainy.

Cook them properly

Drain and rinse, then simmer in fresh water with another 1/2 tsp baking soda for 60-90 minutes until the chickpeas mash easily under your finger. They should be soft, almost falling apart.

Peel them

Drain reserving 1/2 cup cooking liquid. Rub the chickpeas between your hands or a kitchen towel — most of the skins will slip off. Don't obsess over getting every one; 80% peeled is enough.

Blend hot

Into a high-power blender or food processor: hot chickpeas, 1/2 cup tahini (real Lebanese tahini, not generic), juice of 2 lemons, 4 cloves garlic, 1 tsp salt, 3 tbsp olive oil, 1/4 cup of the reserved cooking water. Blend for 5 full minutes — longer than you think. Add water as needed.

The finish

Spread on a plate, drizzle olive oil and paprika, garnish with whole chickpeas + parsley. Serve warm or at room temperature. The texture should be cloud-like.
