What Actually Makes Shawarma Meat 'Good'

TL;DR

Great shawarma needs four things: a 12+ hour marinade, the right fat ratio (chicken thigh, not breast), thin even slicing, and a crisp outer surface. Cheap shawarma fails on at least two of these.

Marinade time

Shawarma marinade isn't a quick rub — it needs to penetrate the meat overnight. We marinate chicken thighs for at least 12 hours in a blend of yogurt, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, garlic, and lemon. The yogurt tenderizes; the lemon brightens; the spices soak in. A 30-minute marinade is just seasoning — it stays on the surface.

Fat ratio

Chicken breast is the wrong cut for shawarma. It dries out fast and doesn't crisp the same way. Chicken thigh has the right ratio of fat to lean — about 15% fat — which lets the outside caramelize while the inside stays juicy. Beef shawarma uses a thin-sliced ribeye or chuck shoulder, layered fat-meat-fat-meat to baste itself as it rotates.

Slicing technique

The whole point of the vertical spit is shaving thin slices off the outer crisp layer. Too thick and you get a chewy mess; too thin and the meat is dry. We aim for 2-3mm slices, taken off as the outer layer browns. The next layer underneath then cooks through, and we shave it off when it's ready. The spit rotates continuously so this happens forever.

Crisp

If your shawarma comes out of the wrap soft and uniform, the meat sat in a steam tray. Real shawarma has slight texture variation — some pieces with a crispy edge, some with a tender interior. That's the mark of meat carved fresh off the spit.
