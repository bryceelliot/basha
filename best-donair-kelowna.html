What makes a donair 'the best'?

A great donair comes down to four things: the meat, the bread, the sauce, and the assembly. The meat has to be fresh ground beef with the right spice blend (cumin, oregano, paprika, garlic powder, black pepper) and slow-roasted on a vertical spit so the outside crisps as it rotates. The bread has to be warm and soft — cold pita is the mark of a rushed kitchen. The sauce is where most shops differ: the Maritime / East-coast tradition uses a sweet condensed-milk donair sauce, but on the West coast most customers prefer a savoury garlic sauce. At Basha we make our garlic sauce fresh in-house every morning. And finally: assembly. A great donair is wrapped tight, not sloppy, with everything distributed evenly so every bite has the same balance.

Donair vs shawarma vs gyro — quick guide

A donair is spiced ground beef on a vertical spit — a Canadian Maritime invention traced back to Halifax in the 1970s, adapted from Lebanese shawarma. Shawarma is marinated sliced meat (chicken or beef) on a vertical spit — the original Levantine technique. A gyro is the Greek version, usually lamb or a lamb-beef mix. All three use the same rotating-spit cooking method, but the meat cut, marinade, and accompaniments are different. At Basha we do donair and shawarma — the Middle Eastern and Maritime-Canadian traditions — and we do both with equal care.
